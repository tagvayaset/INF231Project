Almaty

The largest city in the country, which for almost 70 years was the capital of Kazakhstan. Today Almaty is called the “southern capital”, because in terms of the intensity of economic, social and cultural life, it is really a capital city.

Almaty is located in the foothills of the Zailiysky Alatau, so from almost anywhere in the city in clear weather you can see the mountain peaks encircling Almaty from the south. The fact that the city is located on an inclined surface is also connected with the geographical position, therefore, among the landmarks used by Almaty residents, the concepts of “down” and “up” are the most common.

Shymbulak



The Shymbulak mountain resort is located in the picturesque gorge of the Zailiysky Alatau at an altitude of 2260 meters above sea level, a 15-minute drive from the center of Almaty.

History of the resort


The resort was founded in 1954. Since the mid-50s, Shymbulak has been a training base for Soviet skiers, the championships of the USSR and Kazakhstan were held on its slopes. Every year in early February, skiers competed in Shymbulak, playing "Prize of Alexandra Artemenko", 11-time champion of the USSR, master of sports, participants in the 1956 Olympic Games. Anyone who was skiing or at least watched the training of athletes, they know well how much time and effort the athlete spent on lifting. The pioneers had to climb the mountain for 3 hours, The descent took only 15 minutes. Equipment came to the rescue of athletes overcoming the ascent.

Big Almaty lake


Big Almaty Lake, or BAO for short, is a picturesque lake 20 kilometers from Almaty. Almaty Lake lies in a deep crevice formed as a result of a large-scale earthquake.

Visiting Rules


It is forbidden to violate:
Vehicles are prohibited from approaching the coastline without a permit.
The lake is closed for swimming and fishing.
Access to the dam is prohibited unless accompanied by HPP employees.
Campfires, camping and picnics are prohibited.

How to get there


An easy way to get there is to cover the distance by car. A special road leads to the lake. From the city center you need to follow the street. Dulati in a southerly direction to the fork at the GES-2 bus stop, then turn left to the sign "Alpine Rose", then turn left again.

Working hours:


around the clock

When going to the lake, you should choose comfortable shoes, warm clothes, sunglasses and a hat. We recommend bringing water, a thermos with a hot drink and a snack.

Butakovo Gorge


Not far from the city in the mountains, at an altitude of 1700 meters above sea level, there is one of the best places to relax - the Butakovo Gorge. Its slopes are decorated with aspens, mountain ash, barberries and fragrant Tien Shan firs, and the Butakovka River flows along its bottom.

Threat to the environment


Despite the fact that the gorge is assigned to the “Medeu Natural Park of the Almaty City Akimat” and to the Ile-Alatau National Park, territorially it is included in the city of Almaty. In Soviet times, construction in the gorge was strictly prohibited.

Kolsay lakes


Kolsai lakes are rightly called the blue pearl of the Tien Shan. Translated from the Kazakh language, "kolsai" means "lake in the gorge".

How to get to Kolsai?


If you look at the map, then it is a little more than 100 kilometers from Almaty to the Kolsai Lakes. But since the road takes a detour, the speedometer of a car or bus winds up about 300 kilometers.

Charyn canyon


Charyn Canyon is located on the territory of the Charyn National Park, created to preserve natural and geological objects. The national park was established on February 23, 2004. The park is inhabited by a large number of rare and endangered species of animals listed in the Red Book.

Travel Tips


The Charyn Canyon is located in an arid area, so all travelers are advised to take drinking water with them - at least 1.5-2 liters per person. It will not be superfluous to take food.

Valley of Castles

How to get there?


Charyn Canyon lies to the east of the city of Almaty. By car, they get to it along the A-351 highway (224 km). It is a good road for almost the entire length, and only the last 10 km are laid on a dirt road, where you have to slow down. The journey from Almaty to the Charyn Canyon takes 3.15-3.30 hours.

Lake Issyk


In the Issyk gorge, which opens up in the mountains of the Zailiysky Alatau, which is east of Alma-Ata, there is Lake Issyk, the sad history of which is known throughout Kazakhstan and beyond.

Rest on Lake Issyk


The most popular months for a holiday on the lake are July, August and September. However, many people come here all year round, because even in winter Issyk has a charming power. They say that the lake has a real character, its water constantly changes color depending on the weather. When the sky frowns, then the lake turns gray, and under the bright sunlight, the water turns blue.
